What is a MES Application?

What is a MES Application? The definition of a MES Application (Manufacturing Execution System) is software that acts as a control and monitoring system of information for the management of production processes in industrial environments.

Its core functions are to connect, monitor, and control complex manufacturing systems and track production data in real time.

The main objective of an MES is to ensure the effective execution of the manufacturing operations of the production plant, to optimize the production performance and to improve the quality of the final product.

Secure Policy

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish the principles and objectives of the Information Security Management System (ISMS) at Arido Software, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001. This policy demonstrates management commitment to protecting the confidentiality, integrity and availability of information processed by the organization and on behalf of its clients.

Information Security Objectives

Arido Software establishes the following high-level information security objectives:

  • Protect client information, including data and source code, against unauthorized access, disclosure or misuse.
  • Ensure that only authorized personnel have access to information and systems required for their role.
  • Reduce information security risks to acceptable levels through risk assessment and treatment.
  • Ensure timely detection, reporting and response to information security incidents.
  • Maintain compliance with contractual, legal and regulatory requirements.
  • Promote information security awareness among all personnel.

Compliance and Legal Requirements

Commit to complying with applicable legal, regulatory and contractual requirements related to information security, including obligations arising from client agreements.

Awareness and Training

All employees and contractors must receive appropriate information security awareness training. Training is provided during onboarding and refresh periodically.

Continuous Improvement

The ISMS is subject to a continuous improvement process through the application of the tools provided by the standard.
